The Hidden Chemistry of Daily Life
From your morning coffee to your nightly routine, chemicals are everywhere. Let's explore the science inside the items we use every day.
LIQUID SOAP
Ingredients in Liquid Soap:

Water - Main Solvent

Surfactants - Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS), Cocamidopropyl Betaine (foam & cleaning).

Moisturizers - Glycerin, Aloe Vera (prevent dryness).

Preservatives - Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate (stop bacteria).

Fragrance & Color - Essential oils, dyes (good smell & look).

DISH SOAP
Ingredients in Dish Soap:

The main product: Sodium Stearate (C₁₇H₃₅COONa) = the actual hard soap.

Alkali: Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) → makes sodium salts of fatty acids

Additives: Fragrance, color, sometimes abrasives for scrubbing

Harmfull Chemicals:-

Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)

Sodium Laureth Sulfate (SLES)

Methylisothiazolinone (MIT) & Benzisothiazolinone (BIT)
DAILY FOOD ITEMS
Ingredients in daily items:

Salt: NaCl

Sugar: C6H22O11

Vinegar (Acetic Acid): CH3COOH

Yeast used in Bread: C6H12O6 → 2C2H5OH + 2CO2
