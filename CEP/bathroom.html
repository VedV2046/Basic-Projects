The Hidden Chemistry of Daily Life
From your morning coffee to your nightly routine, chemicals are everywhere. Let's explore the science inside the items we use every day.
TOOTHPASTE
Ingredients in Toothpaste:

Fluoride (NaF): Protects teeth

Abrasives (CaCO₃, SiO₂): Clean & polish.

Humectants (Glycerin, Sorbitol): Keep moist.

Binder (Xanthan gum): Texture

Surfactant (SLS): Foam & spread.

Flavor/Sweetener: Mint oils, saccharin
BATH SOAP
Ingredients in Bath-Soap:

Fats & Oils (Triglycerides): Coconut oil, palm oil, tallow - main raw material.

Alkali: Sodium hydroxide (NaOH, “lye”) - reacts with fats to form soap.

Water (H₂O): Medium for reaction

Glycerin: Byproduct, also adds moisture

Additives: Fragrance, essential oils, colors, moisturizers (like shea butter, aloe)

FACEWASH
Ingredients in Facewash:

Surfactants - mild cleaners (SLS-free usually)

Humectants - glycerin, hyaluronic acid

Exfoliants - salicylic acid, glycolic acid (optional)

Conditioners - aloe vera, vitamin E

pH adjusters - citric acid

Preservatives - parabens, phenoxyethanol

Fragrance & color - optional

SHAMPOO
Ingredients in Bath-Soap:

Surfactants - cleaning agents (e.g., Sodium Lauryl Sulfate, Cocamidopropyl Betaine)

Conditioners - silicones (Dimethicone), proteins (keratin, silk protein)

Humectants - Glycerin, panthenol (to retain moisture)

Thickeners - Sodium chloride, xanthan gum

pH Adjusters - Citric acid, sodium citrate
